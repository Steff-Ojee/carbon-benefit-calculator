Article 6.2 Authorisation
Selling credits internationally to another country
What is Article 6.2? Article 6.2 of the Paris Agreement allows two countries to enter a bilateral agreement where one country finances an emissions reduction project in the other, and the resulting credits ‚Äî called ITMOs (Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes) ‚Äî count toward the buying country's climate targets instead of Kenya's.

What does this trigger financially? Kenya charges a Corresponding Adjustment Fee of USD 4.00 per ITMO transferred (Second Schedule). This is paid to NEMA, and 50% of it is remitted to the Climate Change Fund (Reg. 30(a)). This fee exists because Kenya is giving up the climate credit permanently ‚Äî it cannot be counted toward Kenya's own NDC.

Who is currently buying? Kenya has bilateral framework agreements with Switzerland and Sweden. Norway, Singapore, and New Zealand are anticipated buyers under Kenya's GGGI-supported Article 6.2 pipeline (Phase 2, starting ~mid 2026).
‚ö† Private land-based carbon projects are not required to pay annual social contributions to communities (Regulation 29(3)). Community benefit-sharing does not apply.

Legal Basis: All calculations are based on the Climate Change (Carbon Markets) Regulations, 2024, Legal Notice No. 84, gazetted 17 May 2024 under the Climate Change Act (Cap. 387A). Community benefit-sharing obligations apply to public and community land projects only (Reg. 29). The Climate Change Fund levy on non-land-based project earnings is 25% of aggregate earnings (Reg. 30(b)). Corresponding Adjustment Fees for Article 6.2 are USD 4.00 per ITMO; 50% remitted to Climate Change Fund (Second Schedule, Reg. 30(a)). This tool is for indicative purposes only.
